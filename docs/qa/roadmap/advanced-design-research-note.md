# Advanced Design Research Note

Date: 2026-05-08

## External Guidance Applied

- Baymard Jewelry & Watches and Luxury Goods research: keep the experience benchmarked against product visuals, category navigation, filtering, PDP, checkout, account, and site-wide interaction quality.
- Baymard PDP guidance: keep price, availability, delivery/service confidence, variants, and gifting context near the buy area so users do not need to add items to discover basic purchase facts.
- Baymard filter guidance: keep desktop filters persistent, show counts where available, keep active filters visible, and use mobile drawers/chips without hiding selected state.
- Baymard checkout guidance: reduce perceived effort, keep guest/manual checkout prominent, support natural form entry, and keep order totals and recovery issues visible.
- NN/g visual design principles: use scale, hierarchy, balance, contrast, and grouping to create richness. Limit competing type sizes and let the product/media area carry the highest visual signal.
- WCAG 2.2: preserve readable contrast, visible focus, focus not obscured by sticky UI, semantic controls, keyboard access, target sizes, and reduced-motion support.

## Aphrodite Design Rules

- Product media dominates; decorative styling must support the jewelry, not compete with it.
- Richness comes from editorial composition, asymmetry, measured contrast, material details, and dense service signals.
- Public commerce pages should feel premium and inspectable; admin pages should feel quiet, dense, and operational.
- Avoid generic luxury tropes: heavy gold, dark-only palettes, oversized shadows, decorative blobs, and ungrounded competitor claims.
- Keep every CTA, filter, input, sticky bar, and table usable in Hebrew RTL on mobile and desktop.

## Maison Direction, Distinct From Cartier

- Use fine-jewelry maison cues only as category language: lacquer-red restraint, ivory editorial fields, thin ruled frames, product-first imagery, and calm black typography.
- Do not copy Cartier layout, brand marks, exact color usage, campaign composition, product presentation, or interaction patterns.
- Visual complexity should come from layered editorial rhythm, persistent commerce controls, precise spacing, and service/status density rather than ornament.
- Public pages should feel collectible and refined; admin remains an operational command surface with the same accent language but higher density and lower theatricality.
- Keep the buy path explicit at every step: filters, availability, price, branch pickup, guest/manual checkout, and recovery states stay visible above decorative treatment.
